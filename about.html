The Film

Never Let Me Go (2010), directed by Mark Romanek and based on Kazuo Ishiguro's 2005 novel, follows three young people — Kathy, Tommy, and Ruth — who grow up at Hailsham, an idyllic English boarding school that harbors a devastating secret about their purpose in the world.

The Project

This website was created as an artifact for ENGL 1102. It uses the metaphor of Hailsham's corridors and locked doors to explore key themes from the film: memory, relationships, truth, and the soul.

Why It Matters

At its core, Never Let Me Go asks what it means to be human — and whether that question can ever be answered by those who have already decided you are not. The students of Hailsham live full inner lives, yet the world around them refuses to look.
